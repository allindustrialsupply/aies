    All Industrial Electric Supply's Control Guru,
Craig Kehne, Retires After 10 Years of Service
Ten years ago, All Industrial Electric Supply founder Alex Vaysberg invited a colleague, Craig Kehne, to dinner. He told Craig of his plans to start his own electrical parts distribution company. Would Craig join him as his first sales representative?

Craig accepted the offer, he explained, because "Alex took me to dinner."
 

Craig's sarcastic sense of humor was just one of the reasons why Alex asked him to join AIES. "He also brought maturity and a great depth of knowledge," Alex said. "He is always willing to help and is so personable with customers and peers."

As an expert in sales and industrial control, Craig took on a mentor role for the rest of the AIES team. "Craig taught me the industry," recalled Nick Abbott. "He used each order coming in as a lesson, turning even the most rudimentary things into a learning experience."

Craig's eagerness to help others also made him well-liked among AIES customers. "I’ve worked with him for 15-20 years," said electrician Craig Pignati, "and he’s always been a courteous, helpful and joyful person to work with. He knows everything about electrical components, from light bulbs to controls. I’ve pretty much ordered everything I need from him."

After working for Alex and his team for 10 years, Craig is retiring. He is currently being treated for cancer and plans to focus on his recovery and volunteer work. While he looks forward to helping others in need, he will think of his AIES family often.

"I’m really going to miss my customers and the people I work with," he said. "I have customers who have checked up on me since they heard I was sick. ... And of course Alex has been a great friend while being a boss."

While Alex is confident the remaining AIES sales staff are ready to step in Craig's shoes and serve his customers, he will miss Craig as well. "He has been my right-hand man for many years, as well as a good friend and confidant," he said.

"He's the salt of the earth, a great human being," Alex added.

Please join us in thanking Craig for his 10 years of service. You can reach him at craig@aies.com or 650-425-3015.
